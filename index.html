Species Directory (A–C)

Click a species to see details.

Amazon Blue (Pterinopelma)

Region: Brazil

Difficulty: Advanced

Striking metallic-blue tarantula prized by collectors. Fast and defensive.

Antilles Pinktoe (Caribena)

Region: Caribbean

Difficulty: Intermediate

Arboreal species with pink toes, active and visually stunning.

Arizona Blonde (Aphonopelma)

Region: USA

Difficulty: Beginner

One of the calmest tarantulas, slow growing and very hardy.

Bahia Gray (Acanthoscurria)

Region: Brazil

Difficulty: Intermediate

Large terrestrial tarantula known for rapid feeding response.

Brazilian Black (Grammostola)

Region: Brazil

Difficulty: Beginner

Jet-black, calm, and one of the best pet tarantulas.

Brazilian Red & White (Nhandu)

Region: Brazil

Difficulty: Intermediate

Large, colorful, and fast-growing with a strong feeding response.

Chaco Golden Knee (Grammostola)

Region: Argentina

Difficulty: Beginner

Hardy species with bright golden leg bands.

Chilean Rose (Grammostola)

Region: Chile

Difficulty: Beginner

Classic pet tarantula known for calm temperament.

Chinese Bird Spider (Cyriopagopus)

Region: Asia

Difficulty: Advanced

Very fast, defensive, and highly venomous old-world tarantula.

Cobalt Blue (Cyriopagopus)

Region: Myanmar

Difficulty: Advanced

Famous for electric-blue legs, but very aggressive and fast.

Costa Rican Zebra (Aphonopelma)

Region: Costa Rica

Difficulty: Beginner

Striped legs and docile behavior make this a great display spider.

Curly Hair (Tliltocatl)

Region: Central America

Difficulty: Beginner

Fuzzy, friendly species perfect for new keepers.

Tarantula Care

  • Provide proper temperature & humidity
  • Use species-appropriate enclosures
  • Feed crickets, roaches, or mealworms
  • Do not handle often — tarantulas stress easily
  • Always provide fresh water
